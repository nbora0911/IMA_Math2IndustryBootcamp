3. Ages of mass shooting perpetrators.

  1. Boxplot
boxplot(MJ$Age)

  1. Histogram
  2. Binwidth = 0.5 years
ggplot(MJ,aes(x = Age)) + geom_histogram(fill = 'red', binwidth = 0.5) + labs(x = 'Age', y = 'Counts')

b. binwidth = 50 years

ggplot(MJ,aes(x = Age)) + geom_histogram(fill = 'red', binwidth = 50) + labs(x = 'Age', y = 'Counts')

c. We can see the binwidth = 0.5 years it too small and binwidth = 50 years is too big. Neither of the bin sizes capture the trend in the data.

  1. Density plot
ggplot(MJ,aes(x = Age)) + geom_density(fill = 'blue') + labs(x = 'Age')

  1. Comparison Boxplot, Histogram and Density plot Boxplot gives a very clear information about quantiles, mean, and range of the data. However, it is like a black box. Histogram and density plot give information about the distribution. Histogram and density plots provide better visualization of the data than box plot. However, density plot might provide better insight than histogram because the information that we obtain from histogram depends on the bin size used, which could be either too big or small or lucky a good value. I would prefer to use density plots and box plots.
